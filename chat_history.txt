DNA (Deoxyribonucleic acid) is often referred to as the "blueprint" or "genetic instructions" for life. It's a molecule made up of two strands of nucleotides, each consisting of a sugar molecule called deoxyribose, a phosphate group, and one of four nitrogenous bases - adenine (A), guanine (G), cytosine (C), and thymine (T). These base pairs are arranged in a specific sequence that determines the genetic information passed from parents to offspring.

Think of it like a recipe book with millions of pages! Each "page" represents a set of instructions for making proteins, which perform various functions within cells. The unique sequence of nitrogenous bases in DNA is what makes each living thing's characteristics and traits distinct.
